8K cloud-workflow validation

Prism

Prism is a precision instrument for validating end-to-end 8K video workflows in the cloud. It originates an instrumented stream, encodes and transmits it, and measures the true glass-to-glass latency at every stage of the delivery chain — proving that a full-resolution 8K pipeline on cloud GPUs holds real time, stays inside its latency budget, and holds its picture quality. Because Prism stamps time-zero into every frame, it can measure all of this without guessing.

Precise stages catch the exact frame (frame-level age); freshness stages read the newest served frame — down to LL-HLS partial segments where the endpoint publishes them. A warm-up window keeps pipeline spin-up out of the numbers, and every run resets to zero, so results reflect steady state — not the first noisy seconds.

Capabilities

What it does

A complete test rig in one toolset — origination through playback, with the instrumentation an ordinary encoder can't provide.

Originate

Generate the source

A test pattern, a pre-rendered file, or the instrumented QR-timecode source that carries the measurement. Any resolution up to 8K.

Encode

Hardware or software

NVENC H.264 / HEVC / AV1 or libx264/libx265, with presets, rate control, and split-frame encoding across multiple NVENC engines for 8K. Never falls back to CPU silently.

Deliver

CMAF, SRT, origin, CDN

CMAF (HLS + DASH) to disk, an SRT contribution, ingest into MediaPackage v2 (HLS, LL-HLS, and DASH from one chunk set), a four-rung ladder (8K + 4K HEVC, 1080p + 720p H.264, per-rung codec strings), and CloudFront in front with signed origin access.

Measure

Per-frame latency

QR-timecode latency across T0→T6 — precise where the exact frame can be caught, freshness where only the newest segment is visible, down to LL-HLS partial segments.

Score

Encode quality (VMAF)

VMAF at the encode boundary with QR frame-exact alignment: tiled native-4K scoring for 8K (no official 8K model exists), current-generation models with banding detection, a live quality ticker during the run, and full stats in the report.

Span continents

Distributed probes

Passive probes on separate instances — including CDN-edge boxes on the US East Coast and in Europe — measure real cross-network hops with clock-synced one-way timing.

Operate & observe

Console + Player + self-check

An always-on console (auth, fleet control, remote campaigns, live T0→T6 graph), a hosted player with deep telemetry, and a one-click instrument health check. Every run writes a diffable JSON + CSV report.

What you put it to

What it lets you test

The questions a delivery pipeline actually raises — answered on real hardware and a real network, with numbers you can trust and compare.

Throughput

Real-time headroom

Can this GPU hold 1× (or N×) at a given resolution, frame rate, codec, and preset? Live speed, fps, and drop telemetry answer it on the actual instance — e.g. 8K60 HEVC at 1.0×.

End to end

Glass-to-glass latency

The true source→viewer delay, with a per-stage breakdown — encode vs. SRT vs. packaging vs. delivery vs. player — so you can see which latency dominates.

Across the wire

Cross-machine latency

Measure a genuine SRT contribution between instances, over the real network, with the one-way timing that only synchronized clocks make honest.

Trade-offs

Codec & config A/B

Sweep any mix of codec, preset, resolution, frame rate, segment duration, and bitrate; every report embeds its configuration and its quality scores, and diffs directly against another.

Quality

Does the encode hold up?

VMAF where the quality is decided — the encoder — since everything downstream is bit-exact copy. Absolute scores on real footage, A/B-relative on synthetic patterns, and the report says which it was.

Resilience

Will delivery survive real networks?

Per-vantage fetch-rate headroom beside every latency number, and an origin tuning story proven under emulated loss — the failure mode where latency looks fine until playback dies.

Integrity

Timecode survivability

Confirm the QR survives the encode at a given bitrate and resolution before trusting any number — a pass/fail gate, not a guess.

Integrity

Known-delay calibration

Inject a measured delay at one stage and confirm that only that stage moves — the correlation check that makes the whole chain believable.

The rig

Hardware & cloud

The validated results run on AWS EC2 — a small fleet spanning three regions — but nothing here is AWS-specific.

Encoder

NVIDIA L40S · g6e

Three NVENC engines drive the split-frame 8K60 encode; runs the headless campaign engine the console relays to.

Mux + packager

NVIDIA L4 · g6

Receives the SRT contribution, packages CMAF / HLS, pushes the four-rung ladder into MediaPackage, and transcodes the 4K / 1080p / 720p rungs — without ever touching the encoder's pace.

Control plane

Console · t3.small

Always on: TLS + per-user auth, the pipeline graph, fleet start/stop, campaign relay, the hosted player, and the instrument health check. Survives fleet power-cycling.

CDN edges

Probe boxes · 2 continents

Small CPU instances in us-east-1 and eu-central-1 decode the QR straight off their local CloudFront edge. They power on and off with the fleet — zero standby cost between runs.

AWS glue

Time Sync · MediaPackage · CloudFront

The Amazon Time Sync Service keeps chrony µs-accurate (what makes cross-machine timing honest); MediaPackage v2 is the managed origin; CloudFront fronts it with signed origin access so the CDN is the only public door when that switch is thrown.

Not AWS-locked

Prism is Python + FFmpeg + NVENC/NVDEC, so it runs on any Linux GPU host. The AWS pieces map straight across: EC2 GPU instances → GCP A3/G2 or Azure NV/NC series; the Time Sync Service → any chrony/PTP/NTP source; security groups → the platform's firewall controls; MediaPackage → any LL-capable origin. Same rig, different cloud.

Where it stands

Validated so far — and what's left

The measurement chain is proven end to end at 8K, through a managed origin, to a real player. What remains extends geographic coverage and resilience — none of it blocks the numbers that already work.

Validated

12 confirmed

Demonstrated on real hardware, with pass/fail gates where it counts.

  • Timecode survivability 600 / 600 frames (100%) decoded through 8K HEVC NVENC — the QR carrier survives the encode.
  • Correlation integrity a known +500 ms delay injected at the encode and SRT stages rose alone (+471 ms and +523 ms respectively), with no other stage affected. The injection gate is fully closed.
  • Encode holds real time 8K60 HEVC NVENC, split-frame across three engines — sustained 1.0× with zero dropped frames.
  • Distributed chain, correctly ordered across two machines and true to the frame: 0.46 s encode, 0.71 s at the SRT receiver, ~2.9 s packaged, ~3.9 s at the packager egress — packaging, not encoding, owns the budget.
  • Managed origin ingest into MediaPackage v2 without MediaLive; the QR survives re-packaging (36/36 decoded by the unmodified probe); LL-HLS with real partial segments and DASH emitted from one chunk set.
  • Per-region CDN delivery CloudFront with signed origin access, measured from three edges: home region ≈3.6 s, US East ≈4.5 s, Frankfurt ≈5.4 s cumulative — the first real geographic propagation numbers.
  • Player closes the chain the Prism Player decodes the QR at playback and reports T6 — every stage T0→T6 measured in one live run.
  • The instrument checks itself a one-click canary asserts every probe reported and every stage measured; its first run caught a real probe defect, and the verdict trail (unhealthy → fix → healthy) is on record in the audit log.
  • Cross-machine clocks µs-synced to the AWS time source, so one-way SRT timing is trustworthy.
  • Sub-segment low latency probes follow LL-HLS partial segments: 1.40 s freshness at the packager egress and ≈1.5 s at the CDN edge and player vantages — measured at the granularity low latency is sold at.
  • Encode quality, frame-exact VMAF at the encode boundary on the current model generation, tiled across native-4K quadrants for 8K, with the burned-in instrumentation masked identically on both sides so the ruler never marks the glass. Live ticker + report stats, E2E on real footage.
  • Delivery resilience, root-caused a real long-RTT viewer stall reproduced under emulated loss (cubic ≈550 B/s vs BBR 73 Mbps at 1% loss); origins tuned accordingly, and per-vantage fetch-rate headroom now stands guard in every report.

Honest by design

Every stage is labeled measured, derived, or not measured — Prism never presents an estimate as a measurement. T1/T2 are measured per frame on the instrumented path; T3–T6 are freshness readings taken wherever a real endpoint serves. When the instrument itself was the problem — as its own health check proved once — the numbers were corrected and the old ones retracted. What it can't observe cleanly, it says so.
